FAQs

Do I need any coding experience?

No, no experience is needed. But you will need to have an understanding of very basic computer skills, such as how to access an email account, how to save images to your desktop, the difference between left and right clicking, and the copy and paste keyboard shortcuts.

You can also prepare for a camp or class by sharpening your typing skills! Fast fingers are a coder's best friends.

Do I need to take Web Development I before I take Web Development II?

Yes, we do recommend starting with Web Development I, unless you have significant HTML & CSS experience.

Do I need to take either Web Development camp or class before taking Mobile Programming?

No, there are no prerequisites for Mobile Programming.

What do I need to bring with me to camp or class?

Every student should have a wifi-enabled laptop and power cord. Friends or siblings can share a computer, if need be. If your child does not have a laptop, consider a Chromebook, a great machine for under $200.

Students should also bring a snack and water bottle. And be sure to set up an email account ahead of time. (And know your password!)

Where and when will summer camp be held?

Summer 2016 camps will be held from 9 a.m. to noon at Next High School, 2000 Wade Hampton Blvd., Greenville, SC 29615. Please arrive by 8:45 a.m. the first day of camp so we can check everyone in and give parents some instruction.

Who can I contact with questions?

Email us at hello@code-prep.com with any questions!

Registration and Payment

You must register online at www.code-prep.com. Full payment is due at the time of registration. Your child will not be enrolled in any camp or series of classes until a completed registration form and full payment are received.

Changes and Refunds

If a camp or class series must be canceled by CodePrep due to lack of enrollement, a full refund will be provided. Camp withdrawals prior to June 1 must be received by email sent to registration@code-prep.com and are assessed a $25 processing fee. There are no refunds for camp withdrawals after June 1. A $25 processing fee will be assessed for camp or class changes. CodePrep does not prorate the cost of any programs.

Code of Conduct

Attendees of CodePrep camps or classes are expected to behave with respect to the instructors, their fellow students, and the physical property of CodePrep and its students. This includes refraining from using sexist, racist or otherwise offensive language. Students are expected to show care for the camp or class venue. They are also expected to participate in all camp and class activities and refrain from browsing unrelated material on the internet, partaking in unrelated computer work, including private messaging and social media use, or using cell phones without permission from the instructor.

If there is a situation regarding a student's misbehavior, the instructor will take the student aside and remind him or her of CodePrep's Code of Conduct. If the problem persists, the CodePrep founder will speak directly to the student in order to resolve the problem and will also call the student's parent or guardian. If the problem continues after speaking with the parent or guardian, the student will be removed from the program and no refund will be given.
